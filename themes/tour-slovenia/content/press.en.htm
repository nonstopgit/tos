Media accreditation on the Tour of Slovenia

Tour of Slovenia 2021 will be held from 9th to 13th June 2021 across five stages, namely:

  • 1. stage: Ptuj – Rogaška Slatina
  • 2. stage: Žalec – Celje
  • 3. stage: Brežice - Krško
  • 4. stage: Ajdovščina - Nova Gorica
  • 5. stage: Ljubljana – Novo mesto

Accredited journalists will have access within the official zone in the cities which are hosts at the start or finish of the stage. Accreditations and press materials will be available in the above-mentioned cities, within the PRESS office.

International cycling race Tour of Slovenia is part of the UCI Pro Series and thus subject to strict measures for the prevention of the spread of the novel coronavirus; measures are set by the Union Cycliste Internationale (UCI) and surpass measurement that are set in place on the national level. Their purpose is to prevent the entry and spread of the Covid-19 disease among the competitors and staff. This is why we ask you to follow all of the measures in place, some of them are specified here, some on the locations of the race. To obtain a media pass you will have to fulfill the requirements that were set together with the race media team.

Media accreditation will give you access to:

  • press center,
  • mix-zone in the finish area of each of the stages, and
  • access to the area for photographers/videographers in the start/finish area of the stages (in combination with the vest provided by the race organizers).

Below is the application for accreditation, which we kindly ask you to fill out until 4th June 2021, it will not be possible after this date.

The last part of the document also contains instructions for self-assessment in case of symptoms and signs of SARS CoV-2 infection. You can do a risk assessment yourself. Summary of the protocol (long version is available by clicking on the document link above): Media accreditation will be issued only if all safety regulations against the spread of Covid-19 are in place. The main security pillar against the entry of the infection with SARS CoV-2 is to provide access only to completely healthy individuals (confirmed by PCR tests), who will respect all personal protective measures and prevent the introduction/spread of the infection during the race.

PCR tests
PCR tests for the presence of SARS CoV-2 in the nasopharyngeal space are the basis of protection against the introduction of infection in order to detect asymptomatic vectors of the virus. The entire protocol of the Union Cycliste Internationale (UCI) is based on testing of all people that enter the protective bubble. To obtain accreditation, which allows access to the mixed zone and potential contact with athletes or team members can only be obtained by submitting a negative PCR test for the presence of SARS CoV-2.

  • The test must be taken 6 and 3 days before arriving at the race venue – valid in case of permanent residence in the bubble (= no exits/going home and contact with people outside the bubble for the duration of the race).
  • 1x PCR test 3 (2) days before arrival at the venue – valid only in case of presence on only one stage (in case of a return, a negative PCR test result is required!).
  • 1x PCR test 3 days before arrival at the venue and 1x PCR test after the 2nd stage of the race – valid in case of presence on all stages with intermediate bubble leaving (e.g. home exits after each stage).
  • in case of vaccination against COVID-19, only 1x PCR test performed 3 days before arrival at the race venue is required.
  • in case you were diagnosed with COVID-19 in the past, only 1x PCR test is required (proof of a positive PCR test result older than 10 days, unless the doctor decides otherwise, but not older than six months, or a doctor's certificate that the person has survived COVID-19 and no more than six months have elapsed since the onset of symptoms).
